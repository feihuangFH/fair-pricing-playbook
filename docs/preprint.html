The Fair Pricing Playbook

A practical framework for developing, evaluating, and auditing fair algorithmic pricing systems

Author

Fei Huang

Published

June 16, 2026

Abstract

Algorithmic pricing is now common in insurance and financial services, but practical guidance for translating fairness principles into deployable workflows remains fragmented. This preprint presents The Fair Pricing Playbook, a four-step framework that links fairness definition, model design, welfare assessment, and post-deployment audit into one end-to-end process. The framework centers the anti-discrimination lens while keeping actuarial fairness, solidarity, and consumer trust visible in governance decisions. Three technical case studies illustrate implementation choices and trade-offs in insurance settings. The goal is operational clarity: how to choose a fairness criterion, map it to modeling interventions, evaluate market consequences, and run statistically valid compliance audits.

Keywords

Algorithmic pricing, insurance, fairness, anti-discrimination, welfare analysis, audit, responsible AI.

1. Motivation

AI-driven pricing can improve prediction and segmentation, but it can also amplify proxy discrimination and make decisions harder to scrutinize. In regulated markets, model quality is not enough. Firms also need defensible fairness criteria, transparent implementation choices, and auditable decision protocols (Frees and Huang 2023; Xin and Huang 2024).

Many current discussions stop at one stage, such as model constraints or post-hoc testing. In practice, fairness decisions cascade through the full pricing pipeline and may alter premiums, markups, consumer welfare, and firm profit in different ways (Huang, Shimao, and Khern-am-nuai 2026; Huang and Shimao 2026).

2. Four-Step Framework

The playbook organizes decisions into four connected steps:

  1. Define fairness. Select fairness lens, legal context, and criterion (for example FTU, CPV, DP, CDP), and document legitimate factors.
  2. Design fair pricing. Map criterion to intervention stage (pre-, in-, post-processing) and model design (M0, MU, MDP, MCDP, MC) (Xin and Huang 2024).
  3. Assess impact. Evaluate final-price implications through demand, regulation, and welfare outcomes, not prediction metrics alone (Huang and Shimao 2026).
  4. Audit the system. Apply pre-committed fairness tests with corrected inference and equivalence logic for pass/fail/insufficient-information decisions (Huang and Hooker 2026).

This sequencing is intentional. Later decisions depend on earlier commitments, and auditability requires design choices to be fixed before outcome review.

3. Technical Companion Case Studies

The site includes three technical case studies:

  • Case Study 1 (fair models): cost-model interventions under alternative fairness criteria.
  • Case Study 2 (welfare implications): pricing-rule and welfare trade-offs under demand and optimization constraints.
  • Case Study 3 (fairness testing): regression-based CDP and PD audits with corrected standard errors and TOST-style equivalence testing.

These studies are implementation examples, not universal regulatory templates. Parameter choices, tolerated disparities, and admissible predictors remain jurisdiction- and product-specific.

4. Main Contributions

This preprint contributes:

  • a practical governance-to-model-to-audit workflow for fair algorithmic pricing;
  • an explicit bridge between fairness criteria and model intervention design;
  • integration of welfare analysis with fairness assessment;
  • an auditable testing protocol that distinguishes pass, fail, and insufficient information.

5. Intended Use and Scope

The framework is designed for firms, regulators, and policymakers who need a common structure for technical and governance decisions. It is not legal advice and does not prescribe one approved pipeline for all markets.

The anti-discrimination lens is central, but other fairness notions can apply depending on product purpose, regulation, and social expectations (Frees and Huang 2023; Krafcheck, Balnozan, and Huang 2026).
