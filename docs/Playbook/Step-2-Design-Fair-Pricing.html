Step 2: Design fair pricing

A practical framework for fair algorithmic pricing

Author

Fei Huang, UNSW Sydney

Prerequisite

Step 1: Define fairness. Agree on the fairness criterion before choosing a model.

This step focuses mainly on the anti-discrimination lens from Step 1. It covers how to build models that meet legal and statistical fairness criteria (FTU, DP, CDP, CPV, and related designs). Questions about solidarity, actuarial fairness, and consumer trust still matter, but the technical work here is about compliant cost modelling and mitigation of direct and indirect discrimination.

Case Study 1: Fair cost models provides an insurance implementation (GLM and gradient boosting).

How to achieve fairness?

Once Step 1 fixes the fairness criterion, choose where in the pipeline to enforce it: adjust inputs before modelling (pre-processing), constrain the model during training (in-processing), or adjust outputs after prediction (post-processing). Each approach maps to a standard model design (M0–MC below).

Implement anti-discrimination strategies at pre-processing, in-processing, or post-processing, linked to the fairness criteria from Step 1 (Xin and Huang 2024).

For practitioners

This step describes standard anti-discrimination model designs, not a single approved pipeline for every market. Typical decisions include:

  • Which criterion from Step 1 is binding (CDP, DP, FTU, and so on)
  • Which variables are legitimate rating factors under local law
  • Which intervention stage fits your production system (pre-, in-, or post-processing)
  • Whether to retain a baseline model (M0) for welfare and audit comparisons in Steps 3–4

The case study shows one insurance implementation. Adapt data, variables, and packages to your jurisdiction.

Mini-glossary

Model labels follow Xin and Huang (2024). M denotes a pricing model. DP and CDP are the fairness criteria demographic parity and conditional demographic parity.

Label Plain language
M0 (full model) Baseline that uses all variables, including protected attributes - kept for comparison, not for deployment when discrimination is prohibited
MU (unawareness) Model trained without the protected attribute. Targets fairness through unawareness (FTU)
MDP (demographic parity) Model targeting demographic parity (DP): fit on debiased non-protected predictors X_{NP}^{\star} so average predicted outcomes are approximately equal across protected groups
MCDP (conditional demographic parity) Model targeting conditional demographic parity (CDP): fit on legitimate predictors plus debiased non-legitimate predictors - group differences are allowed only through legitimate variables
MC (CPV) Full model (M0) fitted, then predictions averaged over protected groups at scoring time. Targets controlling for the protected variable (CPV)
Pre-processing Change inputs before model training (used by MDP and MCDP)
Post-processing Change outputs after model training (used by MC)

Model–criterion mapping

Model Fairness criterion Approach
M0 (full) (baseline) Uses X_P and X_{NP}
MU (unawareness) FTU \hat{Y} = f(X_{NP}) only
MDP (demographic parity) DP Pre-process all X_{NP} to X_{NP}^{\star}, removing dependence on X_P
MCDP (conditional demographic parity) CDP Retain legitimate X_{NP_{\text{legit}}}. Debias non-legitimate X_{NP_{\text{not}}} to X_{NP_{\text{not}}}^{\star}
MC (control) CPV Fit M0. Average over X_P at prediction

Pre-processing (MDP, MCDP)

Both MDP and MCDP use pre-processing to debias predictors before fitting (Xin and Huang 2024).

  • Disparate impact remover. Align group distributions of X_{NP}
  • Orthogonal predictors. Residualise X_{NP} on X_P

MDP debiases all non-protected predictors to target demographic parity (DP). MCDP debiases only non-legitimate predictors while retaining legitimate variables unchanged, to target conditional demographic parity (CDP). Business-justified features that regulation accepts as grounds for price variation.

Insurance example

Legitimate features might include claims history or vehicle type. A credit-based score used only as a proxy for a protected attribute would typically be debiased under MCDP.

Post-processing (MC)

\hat{Y}_{MC} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{j=1}^N \hat{f}_{M0}(X_{NP}, X_P = x_{p_j})

Protected attributes used in training only. Averaged out at prediction, giving better proxy control than MU alone.

Implementation

  • Link each model (M0 / MU / MDP / MCDP / MC) to the criterion chosen in Step 1
  • Retain baseline M0 for welfare and audit comparisons (Steps 3–4)
  • Document pre- / in- / post-processing choice and a reproducible pipeline
